Trail Riding Tips for Beginners: Getting Started the Right Way

So you've caught the dirt biking bug and you're ready to hit the trails. Welcome to the club. Here at Dirt Bikes USA, we've been building bikes for trail riders since 1996, and we've learned a thing or two about what it takes to have a great first ride. Here's our guide to getting started.

1. Choose the Right Bike

This is the single most important decision you'll make as a new rider. A bike that's too powerful or too heavy will sap your confidence and wear you out before you've had a chance to enjoy the ride. For most beginners, we recommend the Enduro 250. At 250cc, it has more than enough power to handle any trail in Colorado, but the lightweight frame and forgiving throttle response make it easy to control while you're building your skills.

If you're a larger rider or have some motorcycle experience (even street riding), the Enduro 550 might be a better fit — just be prepared to respect the extra power until you've developed your off-road instincts.

2. Invest in Proper Safety Gear

This isn't optional. At a minimum, you need:

  • DOT-certified helmet: A full-face motocross helmet with a visor is the standard. Make sure it fits snugly without being uncomfortable.
  • Goggles: Dirt, dust, and branches are a constant reality on the trail. Quality goggles protect your eyes and improve visibility.
  • Boots: Proper motocross boots protect your ankles, shins, and feet. Regular hiking boots or sneakers won't cut it.
  • Gloves: They improve your grip on the handlebars and protect your hands from blisters and branches.
  • Jersey and pants: Purpose-built riding gear is lightweight, breathable, and designed to work with body armor.
  • Chest protector / body armor: Especially important for riding in wooded areas where branches and falls are common.

3. Start on Easy Trails

Colorado has some of the best dirt biking terrain in the world, but not all of it is beginner-friendly. Look for trails rated as "easy" or "green circle" — these are wider, have fewer technical obstacles, and generally involve gentler elevation changes. Some great options near our Carbondale headquarters include:

  • Prince Creek Trail: A mellow out-and-back with beautiful views of the Crystal River Valley. Mostly smooth with a few easy rock gardens.
  • Red Hill / Mushroom Rock: Rolling terrain through pinyon-juniper woodland. Great for building basic skills on varied surfaces.
  • Hay Park: A network of old logging roads above the Frying Pan River. Wide enough for beginners but scenic enough to keep it interesting.

4. Learn the Basic Techniques

Before you tackle any trail, practice these fundamentals in an open area:

  • Standing position: You should be standing on the pegs for most trail riding. This keeps your weight centered, gives you better visibility, and lets the bike move underneath you over rough terrain.
  • Look ahead: New riders tend to stare at the ground right in front of their tire. Train yourself to look 15-20 feet ahead — your body naturally steers toward where your eyes are focused.
  • Clutch control: Smooth clutch work is the foundation of good off-road riding. Practice slow-speed maneuvers in a parking lot until feathering the clutch feels natural.
  • Braking: Your front brake provides most of your stopping power, but on dirt, too much front brake can wash out the front tire. Learn to use both brakes together smoothly.

5. Ride with Someone Experienced

Nothing accelerates your learning like riding with someone who knows what they're doing. They can show you proper line choice, help you if you get stuck, and keep you from getting in over your head on a trail that's too advanced.

The Dirt Bikes USA Owners' Group organizes monthly group rides that always include a beginner-friendly route. It's a great way to meet fellow riders, learn the local trails, and get tips from experienced members. Check our events page or contact us for the next ride date.

6. Respect the Trail

Stay on designated trails, don't ride in wilderness areas or through streams, and always pack out what you pack in. Colorado's riding areas stay open because riders treat the land with respect. Be part of the solution.

Ready to get started? Stop by our Carbondale showroom or contact your nearest authorized Dirt Bikes USA dealer. We'd love to help you find the right bike and get you out on the trails.
