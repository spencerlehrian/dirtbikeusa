Quality First: How We Source Components from Around the World

Every Dirt Bikes USA motorcycle is assembled in our Carbondale, Colorado facility. But the components that go into each bike come from a carefully curated network of suppliers spanning three continents. Here's a look inside our sourcing process and the philosophy that drives it.

The Rotax Engine: Our Austrian Powerplant

The heart of every Dirt Bikes USA motorcycle is an engine built by Rotax, the Austrian manufacturer with over 100 years of experience in powertrain engineering. We chose Rotax because their engines offer an exceptional combination of power, reliability, and serviceability — three qualities that matter enormously in enduro and motocross competition.

Our relationship with Rotax goes back to the company's earliest days. Co-founders Carl Schmidt and Steven McFadden evaluated engines from manufacturers around the world before settling on Rotax. "We tested everything," Carl recalls. "Japanese engines, Italian engines, even some American-built options. The Rotax engines stood out for their torque characteristics and their ability to run hard all day without overheating. That was critical for the kind of racing Steve and I came from."

Today, we use Rotax engines across all four models — from the 250cc unit in the Enduro 250 to the 450cc powerplant in the Moto 450. Each engine is paired with our custom engine mapping to optimize power delivery for its specific application, whether that's the smooth, linear torque needed for all-day enduro riding or the sharp, responsive power that motocross demands.

Brembo and Nissin: The Brake System

Stopping is just as important as going, and we trust that responsibility to two of the most respected names in brake technology. Our front brake pumps come from Brembo, the Italian manufacturer whose components are found on everything from MotoGP bikes to Formula 1 cars. The rear brake pumps are also Brembo units, while the calipers — both front and rear — are sourced from Nissin, the Japanese precision manufacturer.

The brake discs come from Russell, a specialist manufacturer that produces rotors specifically optimized for off-road use. Their design sheds mud and debris more effectively than standard discs, maintaining consistent braking performance in the worst conditions.

We chose this multi-supplier approach for the brake system because no single manufacturer excels at every component. By selecting the best pump, caliper, and disc from different specialists, we build a brake system that outperforms any single-brand solution. It's more complex to manage from a supply chain perspective, but the performance difference is worth it.

Dunlop Tires: Where Rubber Meets Dirt

Tires are the single point of contact between your bike and the ground, so the choice of tire manufacturer is critical. We've partnered with Dunlop for every model in our lineup. For the Enduro models, we spec Dunlop's off-road compound — a tire that balances grip on loose surfaces with durability for rocky terrain. The Moto models get Dunlop's motocross-specific compound, which is softer and provides maximum traction on the groomed dirt of a motocross track.

Made in America: Frames, Exhaust, and More

While we source our engines and some brake components internationally, many critical parts are manufactured right here in the United States. Our frames are the most obvious example — every Dirt Bikes USA frame is shaped in our Carbondale facility using our proprietary tube-bending and welding processes. The frame is the foundation of the bike's handling characteristics, and we believe keeping that process in-house is essential to maintaining the distinctive ride quality our customers expect.

Our exhaust systems, shock absorbers, front wheel forks, and headlights are all sourced from American manufacturers. This isn't just about patriotism — though we're proud to support domestic industry. It's about quality control and responsiveness. When we need to make a change to a component, working with a supplier in the same time zone makes the process faster and more collaborative.

The Bill of Materials: Complexity Behind Simplicity

A single Dirt Bikes USA motorcycle contains hundreds of individual components. The brake system alone — just one subsystem — includes brake cables, pedals, pads, front and rear pumps, front and rear calipers, front and rear discs, brake pipes, and lever covers. Each of these components has been individually selected, tested, and validated for performance and durability.

Managing this complexity requires a dedicated parts department of four full-time employees, plus our three engineers and three product designers who continually evaluate new components and potential suppliers. It's a significant investment for a company our size, but it's the only way to deliver on our promise of best-in-class quality.

The next time you throw a leg over a Dirt Bikes USA motorcycle, take a moment to appreciate the global engineering that went into it. From an Austrian engine to Italian brakes to American steel — it's all there, working together, built to win.
