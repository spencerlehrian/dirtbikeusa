2024 Racing Season Recap: Dirt Bikes USA Dominates Enduro Circuit

It's been an incredible year for the Dirt Bikes USA racing team. Our riders brought home multiple first-place finishes across the national enduro circuit, including a thrilling victory at the legendary Barstow to Las Vegas race.

When co-founder Steven McFadden won the Barstow to Las Vegas race back in 1997, he set a standard that the Dirt Bikes USA racing team has upheld ever since. This season was no exception. Competing across twelve rounds of the National Enduro Series, our factory-supported riders secured podium finishes in nine of them — including four outright wins.

The Enduro 550: The Bike That Won It All

The star of the 2024 season was undoubtedly the Enduro 550. Powered by its Austrian-built Rotax 550cc engine, the bike delivered the kind of all-day reliability that enduro racing demands. Riders reported that the liquid cooling system kept engine temperatures stable even during the grueling six-hour stages common in multi-day events.

Our engineering team's off-season investment in steering stabilizer technology paid dividends on the trail. Riders reported a noticeable improvement in front-end stability over rocky terrain and rutted single-track — exactly the conditions that separate winners from the rest of the pack in cross-country competition.

Highlights from the Season

  • Round 3, Moab, Utah: Team rider Kyle Jennings took the overall win in the Pro class, finishing nearly two minutes ahead of the second-place competitor. The red-rock desert terrain played to the Enduro 550's strengths, with its Dunlop off-road tires providing exceptional grip on the loose sandstone.
  • Round 7, Leadville, Colorado: Racing at over 10,000 feet of elevation in our home state, the Dirt Bikes USA team swept the podium. The high-altitude performance of the Rotax engine was a key differentiator, as several competing machines struggled with the thin air.
  • Round 11, Barstow to Las Vegas: In a full-circle moment for the company, team captain Marcus Webb rode his Enduro 550 to a first-place finish on the same course where Steven McFadden made Dirt Bikes USA famous nearly three decades ago.

Looking Ahead to 2025

With the 2024 season in the books, our design and engineering teams are already hard at work on updates for the 2025 model year. While we can't reveal details just yet, expect improvements to the suspension platform and some weight savings that will make the Enduro 550 even more competitive in the tightest single-track sections.

We also plan to expand our racing program to include more support for privateer riders. The Dirt Bikes USA Owners' Group has produced several talented amateurs who are ready to step up to semi-pro competition, and we want to give them the best possible platform to succeed.

Thank you to every fan, dealer, and team member who made this season possible. We'll see you on the trail in 2025.
