Dirt Bikes USA Owners' Group: Year-End Ride Recap

Over 200 members of the Dirt Bikes USA Owners' Group came together on December 9th for our annual year-end group ride through the Colorado Rockies. It was the largest turnout in the event's seven-year history, and the weather cooperated with crisp blue skies and temperatures in the low 40s — perfect for riding.

Three Routes, One Community

This year we offered three route options to accommodate riders of all levels:

  • Green Route (Beginner, 18 miles): A scenic loop through the lower Crystal River Valley on wide, well-maintained forest roads. Led by Owners' Group coordinator Maria Delgado, this route was perfect for newer riders and those who wanted a more relaxed pace. About 60 riders chose the green route, including a dozen first-time Owners' Group participants.
  • Blue Route (Intermediate, 35 miles): A challenging but manageable route through Prince Creek and over the mesa above Carbondale. This route included some single-track sections, moderate elevation gain, and a few rocky descents that tested riders' skills without overwhelming them. The blue route was the most popular choice, with roughly 90 riders.
  • Black Route (Advanced, 52 miles): The big one. This route climbed up toward Marble, dropped into a remote drainage, and looped back through some of the most technical terrain in the Elk Mountains. Factory team riders Marcus Webb and Kyle Jennings led this group of about 50 experienced riders through sections that demanded every ounce of their machines' capability.

BBQ at Headquarters

All three routes converged back at Dirt Bikes USA headquarters in Carbondale for an afternoon BBQ. CEO Carl Schmidt and President Steven McFadden were on hand to welcome riders, flip burgers, and swap stories from the trail. The production floor was opened for tours, giving Owners' Group members a rare look at how their bikes are built — from frame shaping to final quality inspection.

"This event is one of my favorite days of the year," said Steven McFadden. "When Carl and I started this company, we dreamed of building a community around our bikes, not just a customer base. Seeing 200 riders from all over the country come together like this — that's what it's all about."

2024 Product Roadmap Preview

After the BBQ, our head of product development gave an exclusive presentation on the 2024 model year updates. While we can't share all the details here (some announcements are still under embargo), we can say that Owners' Group members got a first look at some exciting changes to the Enduro 550 suspension and cooling systems — changes that have since been officially announced.

Members also provided valuable feedback during a Q&A session that lasted well over an hour. Several suggestions from last year's session were directly incorporated into this year's updates, and the engineering team took detailed notes on the new round of feedback.

Join the Owners' Group

The Dirt Bikes USA Owners' Group is free to join for anyone who owns a Dirt Bikes USA motorcycle. Members receive invitations to group rides, exclusive access to product previews, discounts on parts and accessories, and a quarterly newsletter with maintenance tips and riding destinations.

To join, simply fill out the registration form on our contact page or ask your authorized dealer to sign you up. We're already planning next year's year-end ride, and we'd love to see even more riders there.

Thank you to everyone who made the 2023 Year-End Ride an unforgettable experience. See you on the trails in 2024.
